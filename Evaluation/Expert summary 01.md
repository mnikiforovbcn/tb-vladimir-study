## Expert summary of the TB Preventive Treatment Cascade report

### Study population

- **7,732 individuals** were included.
- Median age was **44 years** (IQR 34.5–54.8).
- The cohort consisted mainly of **contacts (77.7%)**, with smaller proportions of people living with HIV (8.9%) and homeless individuals (13.5%).

---

## 1. Screening cascade

Screening performance was generally strong:

|Stage|Approximate proportion|
|---|---|
|Screened|~99%|
|Suspected TB|~18%|
|Diaskintest positive|~11%|
|Fully evaluated|~99%|

Nearly all individuals who entered the cascade eventually underwent full evaluation.

---

## 2. Diagnostic outcomes

Among 7,705 fully evaluated individuals:

- **Confirmed TB:** 101 cases (**1.3%**)
- **Latent TB infection (LTI):** 829 (**10.8%**)
- **No TB / no LTI:** 6,165 (**80.0%**)
- **No TB, LTI status uncertain:** 610 (**7.9%**)

Overall, active TB prevalence was low, whereas approximately one in ten evaluated individuals had latent infection.

---

## 3. Preventive treatment cascade

Among **1,710 individuals eligible for preventive treatment**:

|Stage|Proportion|
|---|---|
|Recommended treatment|99.5%|
|Prescribed treatment|71.8%|
|Started treatment|70.9%|

Thus, the major loss in the cascade occurred **between recommendation and prescription/initiation**, not at the recommendation stage.

### Time to treatment

Among those with evaluable dates:

- 77.5% initiated treatment within 30 days.
- 86.5% initiated within 60 days.

However, the reported median diagnosis-to-treatment interval was **−105 days**, which is biologically impossible and strongly suggests substantial date-quality problems.

---

## 4. Treatment regimens

Among 1,214 treatment starters:

- 6.4% received a **bedaquiline-containing regimen**.
- 7.6% received a **moxifloxacin-containing regimen**.

These regimens were relatively uncommon and varied across years and sites.

---

## 5. Adherence and treatment completion

Among 1,155 individuals with dose information:

- Median adherence ratio = **1.0** (IQR 0.96–1.0).

Dose milestones:

- ≥50% doses completed: **79.2%**
- 100% doses completed: **68.4%**

This indicates generally good adherence among those who started therapy.

---

## 6. Incentive payments

Uptake was high for early incentives:

|Incentive|Uptake|
|---|---|
|Screening incentive|89.9%|
|50% dose milestone|72.2%|
|100% dose milestone|74.0%|
|One-year incentive|32.2%|

Median delay from milestone to screening payment:

- **21 days** (IQR 12–36).

Site differences were evident, with Vladimir generally showing higher uptake than Murom and Kovrov.

---

## 7. Follow-up outcomes

Among individuals with outcome data:

- TB developed in **18 persons (0.5%)**.

### Effect of completing preventive treatment

TB incidence was:

- **0.39%** among completers (2/518),
- **0.54%** among non-completers (16/2,974).

Among 1,203 preventive-treatment starters contributing **411.6 person-years**, **no incident TB cases** were observed, giving an incidence estimate of **0 per 100 person-years** (95% CI 0–0.90).

This suggests good protection among those receiving preventive treatment, although follow-up time is limited.

---

## 8. Temporal trends

The report covers 2018–2026 and shows quarterly trends in enrollment, treatment initiation and outcomes. Small-cell suppression affected 10 of 95 quarterly cells.

---

## 9. Site comparison

Three sites were included:

- **Vladimir** (largest site; n=6,318)
- **Kovrov** (n=1,034)
- **Murom** (n=380)

Diagnostic outcomes varied:

### Confirmed TB prevalence

- Vladimir: **1.5%**
- Kovrov: **0.4%**
- Murom: **0%**

### LTI prevalence

- Murom: **20.3%**
- Kovrov: **16.8%**
- Vladimir: **9.2%**


These differences likely reflect population differences and/or operational variation rather than true epidemiologic differences alone.

---

# Overall interpretation (epidemiological perspective)

### Strengths

- Excellent screening coverage and completion of diagnostic evaluation.
- Very high rate of treatment recommendation.
- Good adherence among treatment starters.
- Very low observed incidence of active TB after preventive therapy.
- High uptake of most incentive payments.

### Main weaknesses

1. **Major attrition between recommendation and treatment initiation** (~29% lost).
2. **Substantial data-quality concerns**, particularly:
    - 26.6% date-order violations.
    - Negative median treatment delay (−105 days).
    - 11% dose-record inconsistencies.
3. Considerable inter-site heterogeneity.
4. A sizeable proportion (7.9%) remained classified as **No TB with unknown LTI status**, suggesting incomplete diagnostic classification.

### Key programmatic message

The programme appears to perform very well in **screening and adherence**, but its greatest opportunity for improvement lies in **converting treatment recommendations into actual treatment initiation** and in strengthening **data quality and temporal consistency of records**.
